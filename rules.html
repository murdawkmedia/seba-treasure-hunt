The short answer

What are the essential treasure-hunt rules?

Search only while the case is open, between 9:00 AM and 8:00 PM, in areas currently marked open. Respect private property, closures, people, wildlife, and the land. Do not dig or damage anything.

Always-visible safety summary

Participation basics

Time

9:00 AM–8:00 PM

Those are the published daily search hours while the official case status is open. Stop searching during a pause or after the case is found.

Access

Open means open

Search only approved open areas. Restricted, hazardous, temporarily closed, unreviewed, residential, and signed private areas are off limits.

Ground

Leave no trace

Do not dig, cut trees or vegetation, light fires, move structures, disturb guests or animals, or use trucks, excavators, or aircraft.

Parking

Use designated areas

Park only where the current route identifies public or approved parking. Keep roads, driveways, emergency routes, and campsite access clear.

Finds

Document privately

Photograph a possible find where it is, record the nearest waypoint, and use the private report form. Do not post the exact location publicly.

Cost and conduct

Free, respectful participation

No purchase is required. Follow operator directions and applicable laws, supervise minors, and leave immediately if a location feels unsafe.

Map and waypoint labels

How to read access states

  • Open

    Approved for searching during published hours, subject to current instructions.

    ◆ Open

  • Restricted

    Do not enter or search. The boundary may protect private, residential, or operational space.

    ╳ Restricted

  • Hazardous

    A known hazard is present. Stay out unless a specifically marked safe approach says otherwise.

    ▲ Hazardous

  • Temporarily closed

    Access is suspended. Check official updates before returning.

    ■ Closed
