The 10 Stops

Every photo below is GPS-tagged. Tap any image to open it full-size, or tap its 📍 Map link to stand in that exact spot.

1

Creek Property — The Starting Point

“We started at the Creek Property — got out, walked across the deck and down to the creek. Anyone can walk through it right now.”

What the photos show: A double-lot creek property: an old white single-story house with a covered deck, a firewood-stacked canvas shed, and a sloped grassy yard running down toward the brushy treeline where the creek reportedly runs. A mown double-track trail leads out through a tall-grass field, with the pavilion beach and docks just across the road.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.556236, -114.737043

2

The Beach (Randy's) — Open to Everyone

🔥 HIGH PRIORITY
“Randy just opened it up and did a fantastic job. I had the cash out right here — photographed it to text Randy.” The last confirmed sighting of the loot.

What the photos show: Our own stretch of open waterfront — the beach that's anyone's to use, freshly opened up (and tilled soft — ye sink right into it) by Randy and his crew. It's also the last confirmed spot the cash was seen. A sandy, tire-churned shoreline and floating dock ramps give way to a rough trail into thick poplar and spruce, where old outbuildings hide among the trees; the final frame is the very photo Tim texted Randy, cash in hand, right before it slipped away.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.552278, -114.735404

3

The Back Trails (2–3 km)

🔥 HIGH PRIORITY
“Turned right before the staff area and squeezed the side-by-side in — two, three kilometres of twisting trails, bouncing around a fair bit.”

What the photos show: A rough, twisting side-by-side trail runs 2-3 km through aspen and birch — from an open trailhead into a tight tree-lined corridor, then down past power poles to a campground of RVs and tents before hitting a posted 'Private Road / Employees Only' boundary. Loose gravel, ruts, grassy shoulders, and a spur ducking off into the woods the whole way: prime terrain for something to bounce loose.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.560735, -114.739200

4

The Log Cabin

“We came out at the log cabin — weddings go on there, so we circled the outside on the trails.”

What the photos show: The log-sided Seba Beach Seniors Centre marks Stop 4, with Derby's Lakeview General Store as a handy landmark nearby. From the mown, signposted grounds a dirt trail ducks into the aspens past an old fire ring, opening to a gravel clearing and a hillside view down over the marina and lake.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.561632, -114.737411

5

The Secret Road & the Gate

“Came back out through the secret road, up by the gate. Gate was locked, so we went through the woods by one of the yards off the highway.”

What the photos show: A gravel side road peels off toward the Seba Hub, threading past Derby's Lakeview General Store, a fenced sport court, the Nolan Park playground, a lone barrel sauna, and a glamping dome tucked into the aspens with camp facilities out front. Mixed gravel-and-grass ground, quiet-lane feel throughout.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.559601, -114.737940

6

Vista Lounge / Cliff Edge Overlook

🔥 HIGH PRIORITY
“Instacabins, the geodome, the sauna, the wedding stage — then eighty feet down a little path to the cliff edge. I got out there. Wasn't thinking about my pockets, that's for sure.”

What the photos show: Stop 6 crowns the high ground above Lake Wabamun: a gravel UTV trail winds through spruce and aspen to a mown clearing where a big wooden wedding-stage deck (shown mid-build and finished) stares straight down the lake, cream glamping tents and a row of colour-trimmed InstaCabins pitched nearby. A short birch-lined slope drops from the clearing toward the cliff edge and shoreline — mind your footing, and check under that stump.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.561141, -114.739685

7

Kokanee / General Store Area

“Drove through the Kokanee Springs area — the general store, the community hub — on the way to the market.”

What the photos show: An open prairie field lined with a row of brightly painted tiny-house trailers, reached by side-by-side over worn grass ruts, with a treeline along one edge and more structures off in the distance.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.562128, -114.737170

8

Farmers Market Lot

“Drove through the empty lot — the market runs Saturdays. Didn't get out of the side-by-side.”

What the photos show: An open market field laid out with rows of green-screened vendor-stall posts, a bench, a picnic table, and a 'buy tickets' A-frame sign, plus a fenced corner holding a chicken coop, pens, and kiddie pools. On the day these were shot the lot sat empty — no market, no crowds, just bare dirt patches and open ground.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.563285, -114.739381

9

The Driving Range (Kokanee)

Part of the loop back through the Kokanee grounds — the side-by-side rolled right past the range.

What the photos show: The driving-range grounds at Kokanee Springs — mowed tee bays, cutout target boards, RVs along the treeline. And remember the Pirate's Code: no digging up the range, ye keeners.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.563411, -114.739512

10

The Home Stretch — Back to the School

“Back to the school — I reached for my stuff and it was just... gone. Checked every pocket. Backtracked everything. Nothing.”

What the photos show: The loop's end: the RV Pub & Grill with its pergola patio, and the rental office with side-by-sides on the gravel lot. From here it's a short hop back to the old school on Second Avenue — where Tim reached for his pocket and found nothing.

📍 Open this stop in Google Maps → 53.563230, -114.740033
